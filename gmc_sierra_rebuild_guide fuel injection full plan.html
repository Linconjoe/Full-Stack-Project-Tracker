Project Plan: 1990 GMC Sierra 383 Cruiser Engine Swap & Pro-Flo 4 EFI

This project outlines the plan for fundamentally upgrading a 1990 GMC Sierra by replacing its original 350 engine with a new Smeding Performance 380HP 383 Cruiser Crate Engine. This powerful engine is engineered specifically for excellent road manners and massive low-end torque. The fuel delivery system is the Edelbrock Pro-Flo 4 EFI System #35760, pre-installed and pre-tuned by Smeding Performance, targeting an output of 380 horsepower and 450+ lb-ft of torque.

The plan details the strategic installation of the crate engine, integration of the Edelbrock Pro-Flo 4 EFI system, an upgraded 4L80E electronic transmission, and comprehensive reinforcement of supporting vehicle systems. A core principle of this project is to achieve significantly enhanced performance with a smooth, stock-like idle while maintaining the integrity, longevity, and exceptional reliability for long-distance cruising (6-7 hours).

Introduction: The 380HP Cruiser Pro-Flo 4 EFI Vision for Reliability

Understanding the 1990 GMC Sierra's New 383 Engine and Pro-Flo 4 EFI Upgrade

By utilizing the Edelbrock Pro-Flo 4 EFI system alongside the Smeding Cruiser engine — pre-installed and baselined on Smeding's dyno — we ensure superior fuel atomization, perfect cold starts, and advanced self-learning capabilities via Bluetooth. The Pro-Flo 4's 29 lb/hr sequential injectors and self-learning ECU continuously refine fuel delivery to match real-world conditions. This combination guarantees flawless drivability in traffic and smooth power delivery on the highway. We are trading peak, high-RPM race horsepower for immense, accessible torque—ensuring stress-free operation during long road trips.

Phase 1: Original Engine Removal & Prep for Crate Engine

Initial Assessment and Original Engine Removal

The project commences with the safe and thorough removal of the existing 350 engine and transmission. This operation requires appropriate lifting equipment, such as a robust engine hoist or lift. Proper preparation, including disconnecting all electrical, fuel, and cooling lines, is essential before lifting the engine.

Engine Bay & Chassis Preparation

With the original drivetrain removed, the engine bay and chassis will undergo preparation. Because the Smeding 383 is a Gen 1 Small Block Chevy architecture, it will drop directly onto the original factory frame mounts, simplifying installation. We will also plan the routing for the Edelbrock Pro-Flo 4 EFI wiring harness and high-pressure fuel lines (58 PSI required).

Required Specialized Tools (Click to expand)

A successful engine swap requires specialized tools beyond standard garage equipment. Key tools for this phase include:

  • Engine hoist/lift and engine stand
  • Transmission jack (for 4L80E swap)
  • Various hand tools (sockets, wrenches, screwdrivers)
  • Fluid drain pans
  • Wire cutters/strippers for disconnecting electrical components
  • Line wrenches for fuel and brake lines

Phase 2: Crate Engine & Pro-Flo 4 EFI Installation

Key Component Purchases & Estimated Costs for Phase 2

  • **Smeding 383 Cruiser Crate Engine:** Engineered for high vacuum (power brakes) and a smooth, non-lopey idle.
  • **Edelbrock Pro-Flo 4 EFI System #35760 (+$2,415.00):** Pre-installed and baseline-tuned by Smeding Performance. Includes throttle body, 29 lb/hr injectors, ECU, distributor, fuel rails, MAP/TPS/IAT sensors, wideband O2, and labeled plug-and-play harness.
  • **Ceramic Coated Long Tube Headers:** (e.g., Summit Racing or Hedman for 88-98 OBS fitment)
  • **Engine Mounts, Hoses, Fluids:** ~$500 - $1000

Smeding 383 Cruiser Installation (Details)

Goal:

Safely install the pre-assembled Smeding 383 Cruiser crate engine into the vehicle's engine bay.

Key Steps:

  • Lowering the engine onto the factory OBS frame mounts.
  • Installing the Ceramic Coated Long Tube Headers for maximum low-end torque.
  • Installing front accessory drive (water pump, alternator, power steering).
  • Connecting coolant hoses and filling the system.

Edelbrock Pro-Flo 4 EFI Connection & Setup (Details)

Goal:

Connect the pre-installed Pro-Flo 4 system to vehicle power, fuel, and exhaust — system arrives dyno-baselined by Smeding.

Connection Steps:

  • Connect the plug-and-play labeled EFI wiring harness to the vehicle's ignition, battery, and accessory circuits.
  • Plumb high-pressure -6AN feed and return fuel lines to the Pro-Flo 4 fuel rails (58 PSI operating pressure).
  • Fabrication: Weld M18x1.5 bung into the header collector at 15-degree angle for the included Wideband O2 sensor.
  • Mount the Edelbrock Pro-Flo 4 ECU in a protected, heat-shielded location in the cab or firewall.
  • Download the free Edelbrock E-Tuner 4 app (iOS/Android) and pair via Bluetooth to the ECU for initial startup calibration.

Phase 3: Driveline & Cooling Systems Upgrades

Increased horsepower means more heat and stress. Upgrading the cooling system is essential for thermal stability during 6-7 hour trips. The driveline must be robustly reinforced with a 4L80E electronic transmission to handle the 450 lb-ft of torque reliably.

Driveline Upgrades (4L80E Swap) (Details)

Goal:

Reliably transfer torque to the wheels using a robust, modern electronic overdrive transmission.

Components (New/Refurbished):

  • **Transmission (4L80E):** Upgrade to a heavy-duty 4L80E electronic overdrive transmission. This ensures maximum durability on long road trips.
  • **Torque Converter:** 1,600-2,000 RPM stall converter. Because the Smeding Cruiser has a mild cam, a near-stock converter keeps stop-and-go traffic feeling tight.
  • **Transmission Control:** Standalone 4L80E Transmission Control Module (TCM) — controls shift points, lockup, and line pressure. (Specific TCM TBD — options include TCI EZ-TCU or similar standalone units.)
  • **Driveshaft:** Modifications or a custom driveshaft may be required to accommodate the length of the 4L80E.
  • **Rear End:** Consider upgrading to a Limited-Slip Differential (LSD) / Posi Unit for improved traction.

Cooling System (Details)

  • **Radiator:** 3-row aluminum radiator critical for efficient heat dissipation.
  • **Fans:** Dual electric fans controlled by the Edelbrock Pro-Flo 4 ECU fan output for consistent cooling in traffic.
  • **High-Flow Water Pump:** Ensures adequate coolant circulation.

Alternator Upgrade (Details)

  • **High-Output Alternator:** Upgrade from stock to a 100-140+ amp alternator to power the Pro-Flo 4 EFI system, TCM, and electric fans.
  • **Heavy-Duty Wiring:** Upgrade the main power wire to a 4-gauge or 2-gauge to handle increased current safely.

Phase 4: Initial Startup & Advanced Tuning

Pro-Flo 4 E-Tuner App Setup & Self-Learning Calibration (Details)

Startup & Tuning Steps:

  • **Initial Startup:** System arrives with a Smeding Performance baseline calibration. First startup uses that map — no laptop or handheld required.
  • **Bluetooth Pairing:** Download the free Edelbrock E-Tuner 4 app (iOS or Android) and pair via Bluetooth to the Pro-Flo 4 ECU for real-time monitoring and adjustment.
  • **Self-Learning:** The wideband O2 sensor continuously reads exhaust gases and automatically trims the fuel map. The ECU self-learns idle, cruise, and part-throttle fueling without manual input.
  • **App Controls:** Air-fuel ratio targets, ignition curve, idle speed, acceleration enrichment, electric fan activation temps, and rev limiter are all adjustable via the E-Tuner 4 app from the driver's seat.
  • **Professional Dyno Tuning (Optional but Recommended):** For absolute best performance, fuel economy, and reliability on 6-7 hour trips, a professional dyno tune will maximize the Smeding baseline calibration.
